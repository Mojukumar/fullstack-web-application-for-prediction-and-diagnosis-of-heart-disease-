Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the leading cause of death globally, claiming around 17.9 million lives each year. They include conditions like coronary heart disease, cerebrovascular disease, and rheumatic heart disease. Over 80% of CVD deaths are caused by heart attacks and strokes, with a third occurring prematurely in individuals under 70. Key risk factors include unhealthy diet, physical inactivity, tobacco use, and harmful alcohol consumption, which can lead to raised blood pressure, blood glucose, blood lipids, and obesity. These intermediate risk factors increase the likelihood of heart attack, stroke, and heart failure but can be measured and managed in primary healthcare settings.
